How Meeting Cost Meter works

Effective date: 1 June 2026 · Meeting Cost Meter

Meeting Cost Meter shows the live estimated cost of a meeting. The cost appears as an overlay on the presenter's camera, where everyone in the meeting can see it.

What it does

One presenter enters a private, best-guess table of hourly rates, a default rate for anyone not in the table, and a loaded-cost multiplier (to account for overhead beyond base pay). While the meeting runs, the app multiplies those estimates by the elapsed time and the number of participants and displays a running figure:

  • the total estimated cost so far;
  • the estimated cost per minute;
  • the elapsed time; and
  • the participant count.

Getting started

  1. Add Meeting Cost Meter from the Zoom App Marketplace and open it in a meeting.
  2. In the app's side panel, enter your default hourly rate and, optionally, a table of specific rates and a loaded-cost multiplier.
  3. Click to show the cost overlay on your camera. Participants see the live estimate composited natively on your video.

How your data is handled

Your rate information never leaves your device. The server handles only aggregate figures.

Rate data → your browser only. The hourly-rate table, default rate, and loaded-cost multiplier you enter are stored only in your own browser's localStorage. They are never sent to our server.

Server → aggregate state only. To draw the overlay, the app sends the server a sanitized snapshot of aggregate cost state (total, cost per minute, elapsed time, participant count) — never individual rates and never participant names.

Nothing persisted. No participant data is stored on the server or kept after the active meeting session ends.

No HR/identity systems. The app does not connect to HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.

For the full details, see the Privacy Policy.

Tips for using it well

  • Remember that every figure is an estimate, not a fact about anyone's pay.
  • Use the loaded-cost multiplier to reflect overhead if you want a fuller picture of cost.
  • Set a sensible default rate so participants without a specific rate are still counted.

Troubleshooting

The overlay isn't showing

Make sure your camera is on and that you've enabled the cost overlay in the app's side panel. The overlay renders on the presenter's video.

My rates reset

Rate data is stored per browser and per device. You will lose your rates if you switch browsers or devices, use private or incognito mode, or clear your site data.
