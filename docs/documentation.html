How Meeting Cost Meter works

Effective date: 1 June 2026 · Meeting Cost Meter

Meeting Cost Meter shows the live estimated cost of a meeting. The cost appears as an overlay on the presenter's camera, where everyone in the meeting can see it.

What you enter: opportunity cost

The figure you enter for each person is their hourly opportunity cost — the value of the highest and best work they could be doing instead of being in this meeting. It is deliberately not their wage or salary, for two reasons:

  • Pay is private. The app never asks for it, and we never want it.
  • Pay understates the cost. An hour of someone's best work is usually worth far more to the organization than what they are paid for it, so a wage figure would make the meeting look cheaper than it really is.

What it does

By default, one presenter makes a simple best-guess estimate — the average hourly opportunity cost per participant multiplied by the number of participants. Meeting hosts and co-hosts can optionally switch to a per-person model that assigns each named attendee their own value. Either way, while the meeting runs the app accrues the cost over the elapsed time and displays a running figure:

  • the total estimated cost so far;
  • the estimated cost per minute;
  • the elapsed time; and
  • the participant count.

Getting started

Requirements. The on-camera cost overlay needs the Zoom Workplace desktop app, version 7.1.0 or later. On older desktop versions the overlay may not appear on your camera — this is a known limitation of earlier Zoom clients, not a problem with your settings. The side panel and your saved figures work regardless of version.

  1. Add Meeting Cost Meter from the Zoom App Marketplace and open it in a meeting.
  2. In the app's side panel, enter your best guess of the average hourly opportunity cost and the number of attendees. (Hosts and co-hosts can optionally switch to the per-person model and add specific people with their own values.)
  3. Click to show the cost overlay on your camera. Participants see the live estimate composited natively on your video.
  4. Keep the app side panel open while counting — the running total is computed in the panel, so closing it pauses the meter.

How your data is handled

Your individual figures are stored encrypted and tied to your Zoom account. Participants only ever see an aggregate total.

Your settings → encrypted, for you. The names and opportunity-cost values you enter are saved on our server, encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, so your setup is there next time. Only you can retrieve it.

Overlay → aggregate only. To draw the overlay, the app sends a sanitized aggregate snapshot (total, cost per minute, elapsed time, participant count) — never individual values and never names.

No HR/identity systems. The app does not connect to HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.

For the full details, see the Privacy Policy.

Tips for using it well

  • Estimate opportunity cost, not pay — the value of what each person would otherwise be doing. It is usually higher than salary, and it is the honest cost of the meeting.
  • Remember that every figure is an estimate, not a fact about anyone's pay.
  • Set a sensible default so people you have not listed are still counted.

Troubleshooting

The overlay isn't showing

Make sure your camera is on and that you've enabled the cost overlay in the app's side panel. The overlay renders on the presenter's video. Also check that you're running the Zoom Workplace desktop app, version 7.1.0 or later — on older desktop clients the overlay may not render even when everything else is set up correctly.

My settings didn't save

Your configuration is saved to your Zoom account, so it follows you across devices. If it isn't saving, the app may be unable to authenticate with Zoom — for example when it's opened outside a Zoom meeting — and is running with session-only settings.
