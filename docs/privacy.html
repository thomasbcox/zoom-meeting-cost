Privacy Policy

Effective date: 13 July 2026 · Meeting Cost Meter

Meeting Cost Meter is built to protect everyone's privacy. The figures you enter are your own private estimates of hourly opportunity cost — never anyone's pay — and participants never see them.

Summary

You estimate opportunity cost, not pay. You enter your own estimate of the average hourly opportunity cost per participant — the value of the highest and best work someone could be doing instead of being in the meeting. It is deliberately not their wage or salary: pay is private (we never want it), and pay understates the meeting's cost.

Your settings stay in your browser. The attendee count and hourly opportunity-cost estimate you enter are held only for the current meeting session and are never saved on our server — nothing about your configuration leaves your Zoom client. (You re-enter them next time.)

Participants only see a combined total. The on-camera overlay shows aggregate numbers only — never individual values or names.

No HR or identity systems. The app does not connect to HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.

What the app is

Meeting Cost Meter is a Zoom App that displays the live estimated cost of a meeting. The presenter enters a simple estimate — an average hourly opportunity cost (the value of the highest and best work someone could be doing instead of being in the meeting, not their pay) and the number of attendees. The app accrues the cost over the elapsed time to produce a running figure, which can be shown as an overlay on the presenter's camera feed.

The figures you enter

You enter your own estimate of the average hourly opportunity cost per participant, plus the number of attendees. These figures:

  • are your own estimates of opportunity cost, never actual pay — we do not ask for, and do not want, anyone's wage or salary;
  • are held only in your browser for the current meeting session and are never saved on our server (see What our server stores);
  • are never shown to other meeting participants; and
  • reset when the session ends — there is nothing stored to retrieve or delete.

What our server stores

Our server does not store your configuration. The attendee count, hourly estimate, and display settings you enter live only in your browser during the meeting and are never sent to or saved by our server. There is no per-account store, no names, and no per-person values kept anywhere on our side. We do not build profiles and do not track individuals across meetings.

The only data our server records is minimal operational telemetry — see Operational logging below — which is minimized at the source so it is not intentionally populated with participant personal data or your opportunity-cost figures.

The on-camera total

The live total on the overlay is computed inside the Zoom client and passed straight to your camera view through Zoom's in-client app message channel. It is an aggregate only — the running total, the cost per minute, the elapsed time, and the participant count — with no individual values and no names. This snapshot does not go to our server, and nothing about it is stored.

What the app does not do

  • It does not integrate with HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.
  • It does not ask for or read anyone's wage, salary, or compensation data — the figures are the presenter's own opportunity-cost estimates.
  • It does not record audio, video, chat, or meeting content.
  • It does not sell data or use it for advertising.

Information from Zoom

To run inside a meeting, the app uses the Zoom Apps SDK to obtain the in-meeting context it needs to render the overlay (such as the running context and the presenter's own camera identity) and, where required, an OAuth authorization token to install the app. This information is used in real time and is not used to build any stored per-user record.

Data retention

We keep no saved configuration — your settings exist only during the active meeting session. The live overlay totals likewise exist only during the meeting and are not stored afterward. Operational logs (see below) are retained by our hosting provider for a limited period for reliability; they are minimized at the source and are not intentionally populated with your figures or participant personal data.

Operational logging

To keep the app working reliably, our server records two kinds of operational log to our hosting provider (Railway): request lines — the HTTP method and path of requests (for example POST /api/log), with query strings stripped — and client-reported diagnostics and errors sent to the /api/log endpoint. Those diagnostics are minimized at the source: the in-Zoom diagnostics probe transmits only the shape of Zoom data (field names and counts), never its values, and error reports carry a fixed set of technical fields — the error text and stack, the source file/line, the request path, and the browser user agent. Because the endpoint records the body the client sends, we cannot guarantee it is entirely free of personal data; it is not intentionally populated with your opportunity-cost figures or participant personal data. These logs are retained for a limited period and are accessible only to the operator.

Removing the app and your data

You can remove Meeting Cost Meter at any time from the Zoom App Marketplace. Because the app stores no configuration for your account, there is nothing further to delete. If you have any question about your data, email us at thomas+mcsupport@txl-lab.com.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and post the revised policy at this address.
