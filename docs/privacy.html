Privacy Policy

Effective date: 25 June 2026 · Meeting Cost Meter

Meeting Cost Meter is built to protect everyone's privacy. The figures you enter are your own private estimates of each person's hourly opportunity cost — never their pay — and participants never see them.

Summary

You estimate opportunity cost, not pay. For each person you enter your own estimate of their hourly opportunity cost — the value of the highest and best work they could be doing instead of being in the meeting. It is deliberately not their wage or salary: pay is private (we never want it), and pay understates the meeting's cost.

Your settings are stored encrypted, for you. The names and values you enter are saved on our server, encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, so your setup is there next time. Only you can retrieve it.

Participants only see a combined total. The on-camera overlay shows aggregate numbers only — never individual values or names.

No HR or identity systems. The app does not connect to HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.

What the app is

Meeting Cost Meter is a Zoom App that displays the live estimated cost of a meeting. The presenter privately estimates each person's hourly opportunity cost — the value of the highest and best work they could be doing instead of being in the meeting, not their pay — with a default for anyone not listed. The app totals everyone's hourly opportunity cost and accrues it over the elapsed time to produce a running figure, which can be shown as an overlay on the presenter's camera feed.

The figures you enter

For each person, you enter your own estimate of their hourly opportunity cost. You can also set a default for anyone you have not listed. These figures:

  • are your own estimates of opportunity cost, never actual pay — we do not ask for, and do not want, anyone's wage or salary;
  • are saved on our server, encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, so your setup persists across your sessions and devices;
  • can be retrieved only by you, through your own Zoom account;
  • are never shown to other meeting participants; and
  • can be deleted at any time (see Removing the app and your data).

What our server stores

Our server stores one thing: your saved configuration. The names and opportunity-cost values you enter are sent to our server and kept encrypted at rest, tied to your Zoom account, so your setup is there next time. This is the only place individual values are kept, and only you can retrieve them. We do not build profiles and do not track individuals across meetings.

The on-camera total

The live total on the overlay is computed inside the Zoom client and passed straight to your camera view through Zoom's in-client app message channel. It is an aggregate only — the running total, the cost per minute, the elapsed time, and the participant count — with no individual values and no names. This snapshot does not go to our server, and nothing about it is stored.

What the app does not do

  • It does not integrate with HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.
  • It does not ask for or read anyone's wage, salary, or compensation data — the figures are the presenter's own opportunity-cost estimates.
  • It does not record audio, video, chat, or meeting content.
  • It does not sell data or use it for advertising.

Information from Zoom

To run inside a meeting, the app uses the Zoom Apps SDK to obtain the in-meeting context it needs (such as the current participant count) and, where required, an authorization token. This information is used in real time to compute and display the live cost and is not stored on our server beyond the active session.

Data retention

Your saved configuration is kept, encrypted, until you delete it (see below). The live overlay totals exist only during the active meeting and are not stored afterward.

Operational logging

To keep the app working reliably, we record minimal technical telemetry — runtime errors and diagnostic events. This telemetry is designed to exclude personal data: it never includes participant names or individual values. Where the app inspects the structure of Zoom data for troubleshooting, it records only the shape of that data (such as field names and counts), not its contents.

Removing the app and your data

You can remove Meeting Cost Meter at any time from the Zoom App Marketplace. To also delete the configuration stored for your account, email us at thomas+mcsupport@txl-lab.com and we will remove it.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and post the revised policy at this address.
