Privacy Policy

Effective date: 9 July 2026 · Meeting Cost Meter

Meeting Cost Meter is built to protect everyone's privacy. The figures you enter are your own private estimates of hourly opportunity cost — never anyone's pay — and participants never see them.

Summary

You estimate opportunity cost, not pay. You enter your own estimate of hourly opportunity cost — an average across participants, or per person if you use the host-only per-person model — the value of the highest and best work someone could be doing instead of being in the meeting. It is deliberately not their wage or salary: pay is private (we never want it), and pay understates the meeting's cost.

Your settings are stored encrypted, for you. The names and values you enter are saved on our server, encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, so your setup is there next time. No meeting participant or other user can see it. It is not end-to-end encrypted — the running service, and therefore the app operator, can decrypt it, so don’t enter anything you wouldn’t want stored where the operator could read it.

Participants only see a combined total. The on-camera overlay shows aggregate numbers only — never individual values or names.

No HR or identity systems. The app does not connect to HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.

What the app is

Meeting Cost Meter is a Zoom App that displays the live estimated cost of a meeting. By default, the presenter enters a simple estimate — an average hourly opportunity cost (the value of the highest and best work someone could be doing instead of being in the meeting, not their pay) and the number of participants. Meeting hosts and co-hosts can optionally switch to a per-person model that assigns each named attendee their own value, with a default for anyone not listed. Either way, the app accrues the cost over the elapsed time to produce a running figure, which can be shown as an overlay on the presenter's camera feed.

The figures you enter

You enter your own estimate(s) of hourly opportunity cost — an average in the simple model, or per person (with a default for anyone not listed) if you are a host/co-host using the per-person model. These figures:

  • are your own estimates of opportunity cost, never actual pay — we do not ask for, and do not want, anyone's wage or salary;
  • are saved on our server, encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, so your setup persists across your sessions and devices;
  • are retrievable by you through your own Zoom account and are not exposed to other users (though not end-to-end encrypted — see What our server stores);
  • are never shown to other meeting participants; and
  • can be deleted at any time (see Removing the app and your data).

What our server stores

Our server stores your saved configuration. The names and opportunity-cost values you enter are sent to our server and kept encrypted at rest, tied to your Zoom account, so your setup is there next time. This is the only place individual values are kept, and no meeting participant or other user can access them. The store is encrypted at rest but not end-to-end: the running service — and therefore the app operator — can decrypt it, so please don’t enter anything you would not want the operator to be able to read. We do not build profiles and do not track individuals across meetings.

When you end a session, we also save an aggregate past-meeting summary alongside your configuration — the end time, duration, final total, attendee count, and cost model. These summaries contain no names and no per-person values, are encrypted at rest and tied to your Zoom account, and are included whenever you export or delete your data.

The on-camera total

The live total on the overlay is computed inside the Zoom client and passed straight to your camera view through Zoom's in-client app message channel. It is an aggregate only — the running total, the cost per minute, the elapsed time, and the participant count — with no individual values and no names. This snapshot does not go to our server, and nothing about it is stored.

What the app does not do

  • It does not integrate with HR, payroll, SSO, or any employee directory.
  • It does not ask for or read anyone's wage, salary, or compensation data — the figures are the presenter's own opportunity-cost estimates.
  • It does not record audio, video, chat, or meeting content.
  • It does not sell data or use it for advertising.

Information from Zoom

To run inside a meeting, the app uses the Zoom Apps SDK to obtain the in-meeting context it needs (such as the current participant count) and, where required, an authorization token. This information is used in real time to compute and display the live cost and is not stored on our server beyond the active session.

Data retention

Your saved configuration is kept, encrypted, until you delete it (see below). The live overlay totals exist only during the active meeting and are not stored afterward.

Operational logging

To keep the app working reliably, we record minimal technical telemetry — runtime errors and diagnostic events — designed to exclude participant personal data. Diagnostic events record only the shape of Zoom data (such as field names and counts), never its values. Error reports are limited to the error text and a fixed set of technical details (such as the source file and line number); they do not attach participant lists, names, or other meeting-data fields.

Removing the app and your data

You can remove Meeting Cost Meter at any time from the Zoom App Marketplace. To also delete the configuration stored for your account, email us at thomas+mcsupport@txl-lab.com and we will remove it.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and post the revised policy at this address.
